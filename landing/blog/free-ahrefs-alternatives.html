Top 7 Free Ahrefs Alternatives

1. Google Search Console ‚Äì Best for Your Own Site (100% Free)

Price: FREE forever
What it replaces: Rank tracking for your own site

This is the #1 tool every website owner must use. Google Search Console shows you exactly what keywords you rank for and how much traffic each keyword drives.

Key Features:

  • See every keyword you rank for
  • Average position for each keyword
  • Clicks and impressions
  • Click-through rates (CTR)
  • Technical SEO issues
  • Index coverage reports

Who it's for: Everyone with a website. No exceptions.

Pros: Free, 100% accurate, official Google data
Cons: Only shows YOUR site data‚Äîno competitor research

2. Google Keyword Planner ‚Äì Best for Search Volumes (Free)

Price: FREE (requires Google Ads account)
What it replaces: Keyword research and search volumes

Google's official keyword tool. The data comes directly from Google, so it's as accurate as it gets.

Key Features:

  • Search volume data (directly from Google)
  • Related keyword suggestions
  • Competition levels (for ads)
  • CPC estimates
  • Historical trends

Who it's for: Anyone doing keyword research on a budget.

Pros: Free, official Google data, accurate
Cons: Shows broad ranges unless you run ads, no organic difficulty scores

3. Ubersuggest Free Plan ‚Äì Best for Quick Checks (Free)

Price: FREE (3 searches per day)
What it replaces: Quick competitor/backlink checks

Neil Patel's Ubersuggest offers a generous free plan with 3 searches per day. Perfect for spot-checking competitors or analyzing specific keywords.

Key Features:

  • 3 free searches per day
  • Keyword difficulty scores
  • Search volume and CPC
  • Top-ranking pages
  • Backlink overview
  • Site audit basics

Who it's for: Casual users who don't need daily research.

Pros: Generous free tier, simple interface, good data
Cons: Only 3 searches/day (upgrade to $29/mo for unlimited)

4. Answer The Public ‚Äì Best for Content Ideas (Free)

Price: FREE (3 searches per day)
What it replaces: Question-based keyword research

Enter a keyword and Answer The Public generates hundreds of questions people ask about that topic. Perfect for blog post ideas.

Key Features:

  • Question keywords (how, what, why, when)
  • Prepositions (for, with, to, without)
  • Comparisons (vs, versus, or)
  • Alphabetical keywords
  • Related terms

Who it's for: Content creators and bloggers.

Pros: Excellent for content ideas, visual interface, free tier
Cons: No search volumes, only 3 searches/day

5. Google Trends ‚Äì Best for Trend Analysis (Free)

Price: FREE forever
What it replaces: Trend validation and seasonality checks

See if a keyword is growing, declining, or seasonal. Essential for validating whether a keyword is worth targeting.

Key Features:

  • Search interest over time
  • Compare up to 5 keywords
  • Geographic interest
  • Related topics and queries
  • Real-time trending searches

Who it's for: Anyone validating keyword trends before creating content.

Pros: Free, real-time data, great for trend validation
Cons: No exact search volumes (relative interest only)

6. Screaming Frog SEO Spider (Free) ‚Äì Best for Technical SEO

Price: FREE (up to 500 URLs)
What it replaces: Site audits and technical SEO analysis

A desktop tool that crawls your website and finds technical SEO issues like broken links, duplicate content, and missing meta tags.

Key Features:

  • Crawl up to 500 URLs for free
  • Find broken links and redirects
  • Check meta titles and descriptions
  • Analyze page titles, headings, and word count
  • Generate XML sitemaps

Who it's for: Technical SEO audits on small-to-medium sites.

Pros: Powerful, professional tool, free up to 500 pages
Cons: Desktop-only, steeper learning curve

7. Moz Link Explorer (Free) ‚Äì Best for Backlink Basics

Price: FREE (10 queries per month)
What it replaces: Basic backlink checking

Moz's free plan gives you 10 link queries per month. Not much, but enough to check your own backlinks or analyze a few competitors.

Key Features:

  • 10 free queries per month
  • Domain Authority (DA) scores
  • Top 50 backlinks per domain
  • Linking domains overview
  • Spam score

Who it's for: Occasional backlink checking.

Pros: Trusted DA metric, free tier available
Cons: Only 10 queries/month, limited to top 50 links

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really do SEO with only free tools?

Yes. Many successful bloggers and indie hackers built six-figure sites using only Google Search Console + Google Keyword Planner + Google Trends. It takes more time and manual work, but it's completely doable.

What's the biggest limitation of free SEO tools?

Backlink data. Free tools give you very limited backlink information. If link building is your main strategy, you'll eventually need a paid tool like Ahrefs ($129/mo), Majestic ($49/mo), or Moz Pro ($99/mo).

Which free tool is most important?

Google Search Console‚Äîno question. It's the only tool that shows you EXACTLY what keywords you rank for and how much traffic you're getting. Every other tool is optional, but GSC is mandatory.

How do free tools compare to Ahrefs?

Free tools give you 70-80% of Ahrefs' functionality but take 3-5x longer to use. Ahrefs has better data, more features, and saves you time. But if you're on a $0 budget, free tools are more than enough to build a profitable SEO strategy.

Should I pay for Ubersuggest or stick with the free plan?

If you're doing SEO regularly (multiple times per week), Ubersuggest Pro at $29/month is worth it. You get unlimited searches, rank tracking, and better backlink data. But if you only research keywords occasionally, the free plan (3 searches/day) is fine.
