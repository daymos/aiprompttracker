SEMrush Free Alternative: 8 Budget SEO Tools

Can't justify $140/month for SEMrush? Here are 8 free and affordable alternatives that deliver solid keyword research, rank tracking, and SEO insights on a budget.

Let's be honest: SEMrush at $140/month is expensive. Especially if you're just starting out, running a side project, or bootstrapping a startup.

The good news? You don't need to spend hundreds of dollars to do effective SEO. There are excellent free and affordable alternatives that cover the essentials.

Can You Really Get SEMrush Features for Free?

Here's the truth: Fully free tools have limitations. You won't get:

  • Unlimited keyword searches
  • Complete backlink databases
  • Historical data going back years
  • White-label reporting

But for basic SEO needs‚Äîkeyword research, rank checking, site audits‚Äîfree tools can get you surprisingly far. And budget-paid tools ($29-59/mo) often deliver 80% of SEMrush's value at 20-40% of the cost.

Free SEMrush Alternatives

1. Google Search ConsoleFREE

Google's own tool shows you exactly what keywords you already rank for and how many clicks they generate. This is invaluable data that SEMrush can't even see.

What it does:

  • Shows keywords your site ranks for
  • Displays click-through rates and positions
  • Identifies technical SEO issues
  • Monitors site performance in Google

Limitations: Only shows data for YOUR site (no competitor research). No keyword suggestions or search volume data.

2. Google Keyword PlannerFREE

Part of Google Ads, this tool gives you search volume ranges for any keyword. It's primarily designed for PPC but works for SEO keyword research.

What it does:

  • Keyword search volume estimates
  • Competition levels (for ads)
  • Related keyword suggestions
  • Cost-per-click data

Limitations: Requires Google Ads account. Shows broad ranges unless you're actively running campaigns. No rank tracking or backlink data.

3. Answer The PublicFREE (3/day)

Great for finding question-based keywords people are actually asking. Perfect for blog content ideas.

What it does:

  • Shows questions people ask about your keyword
  • Displays related searches and prepositions
  • Visualizes keyword relationships

Limitations: Free version limited to 3 searches per day. No search volume data. Paid plan required for unlimited use.

4. Google TrendsFREE

Shows you keyword trend data over time. Great for identifying seasonal keywords and rising topics.

What it does:

  • Keyword popularity trends
  • Regional interest comparison
  • Related queries and topics
  • Seasonal patterns

Limitations: Relative data only (no absolute search volumes). Limited to general trends, not precise competitive analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a 100% free alternative to SEMrush?

Yes, but it requires using multiple tools: Google Search Console (your own rankings), Google Keyword Planner (search volumes), Google Trends (trends), and Answer The Public (3 free searches/day for keyword ideas). You won't get competitor analysis or backlink data, but it's enough for basic SEO.

What's the cheapest paid SEMrush alternative?

Ubersuggest at $29/month is the cheapest full-featured alternative. It includes keyword research, rank tracking, backlink analysis, and site audits. While it has less data than SEMrush, it's perfect for solo entrepreneurs and small sites.

Can I use Google Search Console instead of SEMrush?

Partially. Google Search Console is free and excellent for tracking YOUR site's performance, but it doesn't offer competitor research, backlink analysis, or paid keyword data. Use GSC for your own site and add Ubersuggest ($29/mo) or Keywords.chat ($59/mo) for competitor insights.

Do free SEO tools give accurate data?

Google's free tools (Search Console, Keyword Planner, Trends) provide 100% accurate data because they come directly from Google. Third-party free tools like Ubersuggest's free plan or AnswerThePublic have limitations but are still useful for early-stage research.

When should I upgrade from free to paid SEO tools?

Upgrade when you're making at least $200-500/month from organic traffic. At that point, investing $29-59/mo in a proper tool will speed up your growth. Don't pay for premium tools ($100+/mo) until you're earning $1,000+/mo from SEO.
